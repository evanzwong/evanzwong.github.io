Hi, I'm Evan! I'm a rising 4th-year undergraduate at the University of California, Berkeley based in the Bay Area studying Electrical Engineering & Computer Sciences (EECS) interested in VLSI digital design, verification, and formal verification of integrated circuits and/or systems-on-chip.

I'm currently an Undergraduate Research Assistant advised by Professor Pierluigi Nuzzo at the Berkeley Wireless Research Center, where my research involves designing a hardware Boolean Satisfiability (SAT) solver accelerator. Specifically, the accelerator I'm working on aims to accelerate the Boolean Constraint Propagation (BCP) algorithm.

This summer (2026), I'm working at Apple in Sunnyvale, California as a Pixel IP Design Intern in the Silicon Engineering Group on the Pixel IP Team.

This past summer, I worked at Crossbar Inc., a semiconductor startup based in Santa Clara that specializes in a type of non-volatile memory (ReRAM) and secure processing units, as an ASIC Engineering Intern. My work involved working with the Design team on integrating scalable, open-sourced RISC-V design and verification toolchains using SpinalHDL in the company's system-on-chip design flow.

Outside of career-oriented things, I enjoy playing video games (LoL, Pokémon, indie games, and many more), anime, jazz, going to the gym, and generally just hanging out with people. Formerly a coach for the Cal Esports Division 2 League of Legends team at UC Berkeley (peak Masters), I currently serve as the Competitive Coordinator where I help form the teams semester-by-semester and support student Esports athletes as the department liaison.
